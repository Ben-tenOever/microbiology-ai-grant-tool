Belasco, Mohr, Schneider

Scientific theme: Post transcriptional RNA control, stress responses, and translation remodeling in infection and cancer.

Matched opportunity: PAR 26 116 Opportunities for Collaborative Research at the NIH Clinical Center (U01 Clinical Trial Optional)

View opportunity announcement on Grants.gov

Example project concept

Working title: Translational stress signatures as shared drivers of viral infection and cancer progression

  • Aim one Define how specific RNA decay pathways and five prime end states reshape host and viral translation during acute cellular stress.
  • Aim two Map convergent translational signatures that arise in advanced solid tumors and compare them to stress induced programs in infected cells.
  • Aim three Test small molecule and biologic modulators that selectively reset pathogenic translation programs without globally impairing protein synthesis.

Example project concept

Working title: Proteostasis and envelope stress as shared determinants of severe bacterial and parasitic disease

  • Aim one Characterize envelope stress response networks in Gram negative pathogens and quantify how they promote survival in inflamed tissues.

Example project concept

Working title: Fidelity errors as tunable levers of virulence and inflammatory cell death

  • Aim one Generate virus panels with graded polymerase fidelity and quantify impacts on replication, population diversity, and pathogenesis.
