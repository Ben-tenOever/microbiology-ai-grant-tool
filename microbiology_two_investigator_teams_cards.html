Belasco, Mohr

Scientific theme: RNA decay, RNA modifications, and viral control of translation.

Matched opportunity: PAR 26 116 Opportunities for Collaborative Research at the NIH Clinical Center (U01 Clinical Trial Optional)

View opportunity announcement on Grants.gov

Example project concept

Working title: Five prime end control of viral and host translation during stress

  • Aim one Define how different five prime cap states and RNA decay activities alter stability and translational efficiency of host and viral transcripts.
  • Aim two Determine how viral infection remodels these pathways to favor synthesis of key viral proteins while limiting antiviral factors.
  • Aim three Test whether targeted modulation of five prime end metabolism can restore balanced translation and limit viral replication.
Mohr, Wilson

Scientific theme: Herpesvirus latency, stress responses, and translational control.

Matched opportunity: PAR 26 116 Opportunities for Collaborative Research at the NIH Clinical Center (U01 Clinical Trial Optional)

View opportunity announcement on Grants.gov

Example project concept

Working title: Stress controlled checkpoints in herpesvirus latency and reactivation

  • Aim one Characterize how distinct cellular stressors alter translational landscapes and stress granule biology in latently infected neurons.
  • Aim two Define how these translational changes intersect with phase one and phase two transcriptional programs during reactivation.
  • Aim three Identify stress response nodes that can be targeted to favor latency maintenance over productive reactivation.
Stapleford, Lee

Scientific theme: Virus entry and cell death decisions.

Matched opportunity: PAR 26 116 Opportunities for Collaborative Research at the NIH Clinical Center (U01 Clinical Trial Optional)

View opportunity announcement on Grants.gov

Example project concept

Working title: Entry pathway and cell death coupling in alphavirus infection

  • Aim one Define how different entry routes and receptor engagements influence early signaling events after infection.
  • Aim two Map which cell death pathways are engaged downstream of distinct entry programs and how they affect virus production.
  • Aim three Identify entry and death pathway combinations that can be targeted to contain infection while preserving tissue integrity.

Example project concept

Working title: Structural logic linking bacterial envelope damage to innate signaling

Mohr, Lee

Scientific theme: Stress granules, translation, and cell death signaling in virus infection.

Matched opportunity: PAR 26 116 Opportunities for Collaborative Research at the NIH Clinical Center (U01 Clinical Trial Optional)

View opportunity announcement on Grants.gov

Example project concept

Working title: Stress granule control of innate signaling and cell death during herpesvirus infection

  • Aim one Quantify how stress granule assembly and disassembly alter innate signaling and translation during infection.
  • Aim two Determine how distinct stress granule states bias infected cells toward survival or programmed death.
  • Aim three Identify therapeutic strategies that modulate stress granule biology to improve antiviral outcomes.
